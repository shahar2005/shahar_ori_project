About the Physics IoT Project

This project explores the application of physics laws combined with IoT technology to automate the task of catching a falling ball. A ball is released from a ramp at a specific height and angle, and a cup moves horizontally to catch the ball using precise control from sensors and motors.

Newton’s Laws of Motion

The motion of the ball is governed by Newton's laws, which define how forces, mass, and acceleration interact. The force applied to the ball is calculated as: F = ma.

Gravitational and Kinetic Energy

As the ball descends, its potential energy is converted to kinetic energy, following the equation: KE = 1/2 mv², with m as mass and v as velocity.

Conservation of Momentum

The ball's momentum is maintained until it is caught, described by the formula: p = mv. The cup adjusts to the ball's speed to ensure successful capture.

Projectile Motion

As the ball falls, it follows a curved trajectory due to gravity. The time and distance it falls are calculated using: y = 1/2gt².

Control with IoT

IoT technology is used to control the movement of the cup, with real-time sensors determining the ball's position and sending commands to motors, ensuring the ball is caught at the right moment.

Conclusion

By combining classical physics with cutting-edge IoT technology, this project showcases how real-time automation can be achieved through the precise application of scientific laws.
